SSIS Staff AI Guide

This guide supports Saigon South International School staff in using AI tools effectively, ethically, and in compliance with our commitment to student wellbeing, academic integrity, and data privacy.

Grounded in our values: Progress, Vision, Integrity, and Love.

The 80/20 Rule

Let AI draft 80%, you refine the final 20%.

Data Diet

Never type student names, IDs, or sensitive info into AI.

Trust but Verify

AI hallucinates. Always check facts and links.

The SSIS "Walled Garden"

Use Google Gemini with your @ssis.edu.vn account for sensitive tasks. Student data is safer here than in public tools.

Teaching & Learning

AI should enhance and support learning outcomes, not replace genuine intellectual engagement.

Human & Social Wellbeing

Consider the impact of AI on students' emotional wellbeing, social development, and sense of identity.

Transparency

Be open about when and how AI is used. Acknowledge AI contributions in your work and model this for students.

Fairness

Ensure AI use does not create inequitable outcomes. Be aware of potential bias in AI-generated content.

Accountability

You are responsible for AI-assisted work you produce or share. Human oversight is always required.

Security, Safety & Sustainability

Protect data, use approved tools, and consider the long-term impact of AI on our school community.

Practice

Use AI to generate ideas, support lesson planning, and create resources, applying professional judgement to tailor outputs to students' needs.

Consider

Ensure AI complements your professional judgement; review and adapt content for your students rather than relying on AI alone.

Practice

Employ AI to support diverse learners, ensuring content is accurate, culturally responsive, and age-appropriate.

Consider

Use AI thoughtfully to provide content suited to your learners' needs, avoiding generic or biased material.

Practice

Use AI to create quizzes, practice activities, or summarise information, ensuring outputs align with curriculum goals.

Consider

Check that AI-generated activities support learning objectives and maintain academic honesty and integrity.

Practice

Streamline admin with AI — draft documents, organise information, analyse data, develop policies, and make predictions.

Consider

Keep oversight on AI-assisted admin tasks, and handle sensitive information personally.

Practice

Approach all AI-generated content thoughtfully, checking for accuracy, relevance, appropriateness, and potential bias.

Consider

Review AI outputs carefully to ensure they are accurate, suitable, without bias and relevant before using them.

Practice

Be open with students, colleagues, and whānau about how AI is being used to support learning.

Consider

Clearly communicate when and how AI has been used, respect copyright, and ensure data complies with privacy requirements. Avoid presenting AI-generated material as entirely your own.

Practice

Use AI to brainstorm ideas, gather explanations, or explore new perspectives, while ensuring your own thinking leads the way.

Consider

Be cautious not to let AI dominate your ideas. Always adapt and personalise suggestions so they sound like you.

Practice

Let AI help you practise or understand concepts, but make sure your final work is genuinely your own.

Consider

Relying too heavily on AI can reduce your independent thinking skills. Avoid using it for shortcuts.

Practice

Draw on AI to get feedback, examples, or practice activities suited to your needs.

Consider

AI can sometimes generate inaccurate, biased, or inappropriate content. Double-check information.

Practice

Use AI to organise notes, summarise information, or create practice questions.

Consider

Overuse of AI for organisation or summaries can stop you from developing your own study habits. Use it as a scaffold, not a crutch.

Practice

Evaluate AI outputs carefully. Check for accuracy, relevance, and fairness.

Consider

AI may give confident but wrong answers. Always cross-check with reliable sources.

Practice

Acknowledge when and how AI has supported your work, and never present AI-generated work as solely your own.

Consider

Use AI ethically, respect copyright and data privacy, and clearly acknowledge its role.
